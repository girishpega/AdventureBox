# AdventureBox · Illustration Style Guide

**Style name:** *Painterly Storybook Realism*

This is the single visual language for every AdventureBox illustration — book pages, covers, stickers, marketing, and app backgrounds.

---

## The One-Sentence Style

> *Pixar characters painted by Beatrix Potter on a Caldecott Honor book.*

---

## Style Pillars

### 1. Character Appeal First

Every character must pass the **squint test**: blur your eyes — you should still feel the emotion. Eyes are always the focal point. Proportions favor appeal over accuracy (big heads, big eyes, small bodies).

### 2. Painterly, Never Flat

Visible brushwork in skies, grass, fur, and feathers. Soft edges. No hard vector outlines. No cel-shading. Textures suggest cold-press watercolor paper and gouache underneath.

### 3. Light Tells the Story

Lighting changes with emotional beat — not randomly. Brighter = joy. Softer = tenderness. Golden = magic. Cool blue = quiet wonder. Never harsh flash photography look.

### 4. Nature Is a Character

Environments are never generic green blurs. Meadows have specific flowers. Night skies have depth. Water reflects mood. The world feels inhabited and loved.

### 5. Restraint Over Spectacle

One clear focal point per image. Negative space is confidence. A single firefly can carry a page. We do not fill every pixel.

## Medium Specification

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Simulated medium** | Digital watercolor + gouache |
| **Paper texture** | Cold-press watercolor, subtle grain, 3–5% opacity overlay |
| **Edge treatment** | Soft, lost edges on backgrounds; slightly sharper on character faces |
| **Line work** | Minimal. No black outlines. Form defined by color and value only. |
| **Detail density** | High on faces and hands/trunk/wings. Low on distant backgrounds. |

---

## Character Rendering Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Eyes** | Always two white catchlights (main + secondary). Pupils dark with color depth. |
| **Skin/fur/feather** | Minimum 3 values: highlight, midtone, shadow. Warm shadows never pure gray. |
| **Expressions** | Readable at 200px thumbnail. Eyebrows and mouth do heavy lifting. |
| **Scale** | Characters feel tactile — you want to touch the fluff, the trunk, the wings. |
| **Consistency** | Same character = same colors, proportions, and features across all media. |

---

## Environment Rendering Rules

| Environment | Treatment |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Meadows** | Specific wildflowers, varied grass height, dappled light |
| **Night sky** | Layered depth — not flat navy. Stars vary in size and warmth. |
| **Water** | Reflective, catches character color. Ripples suggest movement. |
| **Forest** | Soft vertical depth. Background trees blurred. Foreground detail sharp. |
| **Cozy interiors** | Warm amber pools of light. Visible texture in wood, moss, fabric. |

---

## Composition Rules

1. **Portrait 4:5** for story pages. **Portrait 2:3** for covers.
2. **Rule of thirds** — character eyes on upper third line.
3. **Child's eye level** — camera at 60–100cm height unless dramatic beat requires otherwise.
4. **Safe zone** — bottom 15% reserved for app text overlay (no critical detail).
5. **One story per frame** — one action, one emotion, one moment.

---

## Color Application in Illustrations

See [COLORS.md](./COLORS.md) for full palette. In illustrations:

- **Shadows are warm**, never pure black or cold blue-gray
- **Highlights are cream or gold**, never pure white (except water sparkles)
- **Saturation peaks at emotional climax** (Page 4 of every story)
- **Backgrounds support, never compete** — 20% lower saturation than foreground

## Style References

### Emulate

| Reference | What to take |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Pixar short films** (*Piper*, *Lou*, *For the Birds*) | Character appeal, emotional clarity |
| **Beatrix Potter** | Watercolor texture, natural environments, gentle scale |
| **Jon Klassen** | Restraint, negative space, subtle humor |
| **Oliver Jeffers** | Painterly warmth, hand-crafted feel |
| **Disney Golden Books (classic era)** | Premium color, storybook composition |
| **Caldecott Honor winners** | Illustration-as-art quality bar |

### Avoid

| Reference | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Mobile game art (Coin Master, etc.) | Flat, loud, transactional |
| Generic stock illustration | No soul, no texture |
| Anime / chibi | Wrong age signal, wrong proportions |
| Photorealistic 3D | Uncanny for ages 4–6, not cozy |
| Coloring book line art | Too unfinished for premium brand |
| AI generic "cute animal" | No character consistency |

## Universal Negative Prompts

Append to every AI generation:

```
Avoid: photorealistic, 3D render, CGI, Unreal Engine, anime, chibi, cartoon 
network flat style, vector art, clip art, stock illustration, harsh black 
outlines, cel shading, oversaturated neon, dark scary mood, horror, sharp teeth, 
aggressive expression, blood, weapons, text in image, watermark, signature, 
logo, blurry faces, deformed anatomy, extra limbs, duplicate characters, 
inconsistent character design, low quality, jpeg artifacts, lens flare, 
HDR overprocessing, adult themes, human children as focus
```

---

## Quality Bar Checklist

Before any illustration is approved:

- [ ] Would this hang in a child's nursery?
- [ ] Does the character's emotion read in 2 seconds?
- [ ] Is there visible painterly texture?
- [ ] Are shadows warm, not cold?
- [ ] Is there one clear focal point?
- [ ] Does it match the Character Bible?
- [ ] Does it feel AdventureBox — not generic?
